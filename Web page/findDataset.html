Finding your dataset

What makes a good dataset?

  • How good a dataset is entirely depends on the situation and what you are trying to achieve.
  • In our case we are working with images and generally speaking the more images you have the better results you will have.
  • However larger datasets come with the tradeoff that they take significantly more time and effort to work with
  • For the purposes of this guide i would recommend that you keep your dataset below 2gb in size

Note: In order to work with StyleGan2-ada the resolution has to be a power of two, with identical height and width for example: 64x64,128x128,256x256. Either you can find a dataset in this format or we can simply convert it, which i shall be showing you how to do.

Where can i find these datasets?

These are just some of the good datasets/collections of datasets that i have found:

  • Kaggle - a collection of over 70,000 datasets with a search function
  • Chicago faces database - a dataset of high resolution, standardised males + females with varying ethnicity
  • Google - Google's dataset search feature
  • Cats and Dogs- 25k images, half dogs, half cats. This is what i will be working with

If none of these seem appealing you can find even more datasets here

Please note that for the sake of simplicity i would recommend finding datasets with image sizes less or equal to 1024x1024

I've found the dataset i want, what now?

Consider whether or not you will be able to train using your dataset; google colab has an 8 hour limit and so larger datasets may need to be cut down to ensure you dont run out of time when training. in my case i started with trying to use 12.5k dog images but needed to cut it down to only 3k as i kept running into the 8 hour limit.

After this simply ensure your dataset is in a zipped file (if it isn't then right click the folder then do send to>zipped file) and upload this to your google drive.
